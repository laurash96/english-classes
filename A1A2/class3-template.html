First Steps · Class 3 · Present Simple
My Pen Pal Letter
You have a new pen pal who lives abroad. She doesn't know anything about your life. Write her a short letter about your typical day — what you do, what you love, what you hate.
⏱ Writing time: 10 minutes
🎯 Goal: Present Simple — I/she/he + affirmative, negative
✅ At least 6 sentences
1
Each person writes her own letter. Don't look at the other's — you'll read them aloud and react to each other.
2
Use Present Simple. At least 6 sentences. You can use the frames below if you need them.
3
If you don't know a word in English, write it in Spanish and circle it. The teacher will help — keep writing and don't stop.
Sentence frames — use them, change them, make them yours
Every day, I wake up at ___.
Todos los días me levanto a las ___.
I usually ___ in the morning.
Normalmente ___ por la mañana.
I love ___. I hate ___.
Me encanta ___. Odio ___.
I don't like ___.
No me gusta ___.
At night, I usually ___.
Por la noche, normalmente ___.
On weekends, I ___.
Los fines de semana, yo ___.

Teacher debrief guide — highlight sentences in these colors
Green = Affirmative ("I wake up at 7")
Orange = Negative ("I don't like cooking")
Blue = Frequency adverb ("I usually / always / never")

After students read aloud, copy 3–4 sentences from each letter into the board and highlight them. The Present Simple grammar map emerges from their own words — authentic, not textbook.
